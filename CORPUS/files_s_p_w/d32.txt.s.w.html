Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great of Persia Achaemenid_Empire commonly known as Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Great and also called Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Elder by the Greeks Ancient_Greece, was the founder of the Achaemenid Empire, the first Persian Empire. Under his rule, the empire embraced all the previous civilized states of the ancient Near Near_East East, expanded vastly and eventually conquered most of Southwest Asia Western_Asia and much of Central Asia Central_Asia and the Caucasus. From the Mediterranean Sea Mediterranean_Sea and Hellespont Dardanelles in the west to the Indus River Indus_River in the east, Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Great created the largest empire List_of_largest_empires the world had yet seen.Under his successors, the empire eventually stretched at its maximum extent from parts of the Balkans (Bulgaria-Paeonia and Thrace-Macedonia) and Eastern Europe Eastern_Europe proper in the west, to the Indus Valley Indus_River in the east. His regal titles in full were The Great King The_Great_King, King of Persia Achaemenid_Empire, King of Anshan, King of Media, King of Babylon, King of Sumer and Akkad Akkadian_Empire, and King of the Four Corners Four_Corners of the World. The Nabonidus Chronicle Nabonidus_Chronicle notes the change in his title from simply "King of Anshan", a city City, to "King of Persia Achaemenid_Empire". Assyriologist Assyria François Vallat wrote that "When Astyages marched against Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great, Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great is called ‘King of Anshan’, but when Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great crosses the Tigris on his way to Lydia, he is ‘King of Persia Achaemenid_Empire’. The coup therefore took place between these two events."

The reign of Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Great lasted c. 30 years. Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great built his empire by first conquering the Median Empire, then the Lydian Empire, and eventually the Neo-Babylonian Empire. He led an expedition into Central Asia Central_Asia, which resulted in major campaigns that were described as having brought "into subjection every nation without exception".Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great did not venture into Egypt Ancient_Egypt, and was alleged to have died in battle Battle, fighting the Massagetae along the Syr Darya Syr_Darya in December 530 BC. He was succeeded by his son, Cambyses II Cambyses_II, who managed to add to the empire by conquering Egypt Ancient_Egypt, Nubia, and Cyrenaica during his short rule.

Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Great respected the customs and religions of the lands he conquered. This became a very successful model for centralized administration and establishing a government working to the advantage and profit of its subjects.In fact, the administration of the empire through satraps and the vital principle of forming a government at Pasargadae were the works of Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great II.What is sometimes referred to as the Edict of Restoration Bourbon_Restoration (actually two edicts) described in the Bible Hebrew_Bible as being made by Cyrus II Cyrus_the_Great the Great left a lasting legacy on the Jewish Jews religion, where, because of his policies in Babylonia, he is referred to by the Jewish Bible Hebrew_Bible as messiah Messiah (lit.
