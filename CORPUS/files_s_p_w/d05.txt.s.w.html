Thermopylae, Modern Greek Greek_language Thermopýles, also spelled Thermopílai, narrow pass on the east coast of central Greece Ancient_Greece between the Kallídhromon massif Massif and the Gulf of Maliakós, about 85 miles (136 km) northwest of Athens Classical_Athens (Athína). In antiquity Ancient_Greece its cliffs were by the sea, but silting has widened the distance to more than a mile. Its name, meaning hot gates, is derived from its hot sulfur Sulfur springs.

The pass, some 4 miles (6 km) in length, has figured in numerous invasions.
