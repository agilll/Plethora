Alexander Alexander_the_Great I of Macedon Achaemenid_Macedonia (Greek Ancient_Greece: Ἀλέξανδρος ὁ Μακεδών), known with the title Philhellene Philhellenism (Greek Ancient_Greece: φιλέλλην, literally "lover of the Greeks Ancient_Greece", meaning "patriot"), was the ruler of the ancient Kingdom of Macedon Achaemenid_Macedonia from c. 498 BC until his death in 454 BC. He was succeeded by his eldest son, Alcetas II.

Alexander Alexander_the_Great I was the son of Amyntas I Amyntas_I_of_Macedon and Queen Eurydice Eurydice_I_of_Macedon (Greek Ancient_Greece: Εὐρυδίκη). He had a sister named Gygaea Gygaea_of_Macedon

He gave his sister for marriage to the Persian Achaemenid_Empire general Bubares, in the late 6th century BC who was in Macedon Achaemenid_Macedonia at the time, in order to stop him from searching for Persian Achaemenid_Empire soldiers who had been killed by Alexander Alexander_the_Great I's men following his commands.[2][3]

Alexander Alexander_the_Great I came to the throne during the era of the kingdom's vassalage at the hand of Achaemenid Persia Achaemenid_Empire, dating back to the time of his father, Amyntas I Amyntas_I_of_Macedon, although Macedon Achaemenid_Macedonia retained a broad scope of autonomy.[4] In 492 BC it was made to a fully subordinate part of the Persian Achaemenid_Empire Kingdom by Mardonius' campaign.[5] At that time, Alexander Alexander_the_Great I was on the nominal Macedonian Ancient_Macedonians throne. Alexander Alexander_the_Great I further acted as a representative of the Persian Achaemenid_Empire governor Mardonius during peace negotiations after the Persian Achaemenid_Empire defeat at the Battle of Salamis Battle_of_Salamis in 480 BC. In later events, Herodotus several times mentions Alexander Alexander_the_Great I as a man who is on Xerxes Xerxes_I' side and follows the assigned tasks.[6]

From the time of Mardonius' conquest of Macedon Achaemenid_Macedonia, Alexander Alexander_the_Great I is referred to as hyparchos by Herodotus, meaning subordinate governor.[7] Despite his cooperation with Persia Achaemenid_Empire, Alexander Alexander_the_Great I frequently gave supplies and advice to the Greek Ancient_Greece city City states, and warned them of Mardonius' plans before the Battle of Plataea Battle_of_Plataea in 479 BC. Most of the 43,000 survivors were attacked and killed by the forces of Alexander Alexander_the_Great I at the estuary Thames_Estuary of the Strymon Struma_(river) river.
