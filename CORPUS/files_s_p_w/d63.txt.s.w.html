The Immortals List_of_NCIS_episodes also known as the Persian Immortals Immortals_(Achaemenid_Empire) or Persian Achaemenid_Empire Warriors was the name given by Herodotus to an elite heavily-armed infantry unit of 10,000 soldiers in the army of the Achaemenid Empire. This force performed the dual roles of both Imperial Guard Imperial_Guard and standing army Standing_army. The force consisted mainly of Persians Achaemenid_Empire, but also included Medes and Elamites Elam. Essential questions regarding the unit (even its native name) remain unanswered, because authentic sources are missing.[1]

Herodotus describes the 'Immortals Legendary_creature' as being heavy infantry Heavy_infantry, led by Hydarnes Hydarnes_II, that were kept constantly at a strength of exactly 10,000 men. He stated that the unit's name stemmed from the custom that every killed, seriously wounded, or sick member was immediately replaced with a new one, maintaining the corps as a cohesive entity with a constant strength.[2]

This elite corps is only called the 'Immortals Legendary_creature' in sources based on Herodotus. There is evidence of the existence of a permanent corps from Persian Achaemenid_Empire sources, which provided a backbone for the tribal levies who made up the bulk of the Achaemenid Achaemenid_Empire armies. These do not however record the name of "Immortals Legendary_creature". It is suggested that Herodotus' informant has confused the word anûšiya- ("companion") with anauša- ("immortal"),[2] but this theory has been criticized by Rudiger Schmidt.[1]

The Immortals List_of_NCIS_episodes played an important role in Cambyses II Cambyses_II's conquest of Egypt Ancient_Egypt in 525 BC and Darius I Darius_I's invasion of India British_Raj's smaller western frontier Frontier kingdoms (western Punjab Punjab_(region) and Sindh, now in Pakistan) and Scythia Scythians in 520 BC and 513 BC. Immortals Legendary_creature participated in the Battle of Thermopylae Battle_of_Thermopylae 480 BC and were amongst the Persian Achaemenid_Empire occupation troops in Greece Ancient_Greece in 479 BC under Mardonius.[2]

During the final decades of the Achaemenid Achaemenid_Empire empire, the role expected of the hazarapatish (commanding officer) of the Immortals Legendary_creature was extended to include that of chief minister to the king.[3] The provision of a bodyguard, in direct attendance on the monarch, had already been allocated to a select thousand strong detachment of the corps.[3]

Herodotus describes their armament as follows: wicker shields, short spears, quivers, swords or large daggers, slings, bow Bow_and_arrow and arrow. Underneath their robes they wore scale armour Scale_armour coats. The spear counterbalances of the common soldiery were of silver; to differentiate commanding ranks, the officers' spear butt-spikes were golden.[2] The regiment was followed by a caravan of covered carriages, camels, and mules that transported their supplies, along with concubines and attendants to serve them; this supply train carried special food that was reserved only for their consumption.[5][6]

The headdress worn by the Immortals Legendary_creature is believed to have been the Persian Achaemenid_Empire tiara Tiara. Its actual form is uncertain, but some sources describe it as a cloth or felt cap Cap which could be pulled over the face to keep out wind and dust in the arid Persian Achaemenid_Empire plains.[2] Surviving Achaemenid Achaemenid_Empire coloured glazed bricks and carved reliefs represent the Immortals Legendary_creature as wearing elaborate robes, hoop Hoop_skirt earrings and gold jewelry, though these garments and accessories were most likely worn only for ceremonial occasions.[7]

The title of "Immortals Legendary_creature" was first revived under the Sassanid army Military_of_the_Sasanian_Empire. The backbone of the army was the Aswaran, and the most famous of the Aswaran units were the Zhayedan (literally "Immortals Legendary_creature") and numbered 10,000 men, like the Achaemenid Achaemenid_Empire predecessors, with the difference that they were heavy cavalry Cavalry. Their task was mainly to secure any breakthroughs and to enter battles at crucial stages.

The designation "Immortal List_of_minor_Angel_characters" to describe a military unit was used twice during the Byzantine Empire, first as an elite heavy cavalry Cavalry unit under John I Tzimiskes John_I_Tzimiskes (r. 969–976) and then later by Nikephoritzes, the chief minister of Emperor List_of_Byzantine_emperors Michael VII Michael_VII_Doukas (r. 1071–1081), as the core of a new central field Central_Field_(Iwo_Jima) army, following the disastrous defeat of Manzikert Battle_of_Manzikert by the Seljuk Turks Seljuq_dynasty in 1071.

Many centuries later, during the Napoleonic Wars Napoleonic_Wars, French French_Army soldiers referred to Napoleon's Imperial Guard Imperial_Guard as "the Immortals Legendary_creature".[8]

The Iranian Army Islamic_Republic_of_Iran_Army under the last Shahanshah Shah included an all volunteer Javidan Guard, also known as the "Immortals Legendary_creature" after the ancient Persian Achaemenid_Empire royal guard. The "Immortals Legendary_creature" were based in the Lavizan Barracks in Tehran. By 1978 this elite force comprised a brigade of 4,000–5,000 men, including a battalion Battalion of Chieftain tanks Chieftain_(tank). Following the Iranian Revolution Iranian_Revolution of 1979 the "Immortals Legendary_creature" were disbanded.[9]
