Delphi was an important ancient Greek Ancient_Greece religious sanctuary sacred to the god Apollo. Located on Mt. Parnassus Mount_Parnassus near the Gulf of Corinth, the sanctuary was home to the famous oracle Delphi of Apollo which gave cryptic predictions and guidance to both city-states and individuals. In addition, Delphi was also home to the panhellenic Pythian Games Pythian_Games.

The site was first settled in Mycenaean Mycenaean_Greece times in the late Bronze Age Bronze_Age (1500-1100 BCE Common_Era) but took on its religious significance from around 800 BCE Common_Era. The original name of the sanctuary was Pytho Delphi after the snake Snake which Apollo was believed to have killed there. Votive offerings at the site from this period include small clay Pottery statues (the earliest), bronze figurines, and richly decorated bronze tripods.

Delphi was also considered the centre of the world, for in Greek mythology Greek_mythology Zeus released two eagles, one to the east and another to the west, and Delphi was the point at which they met after encircling the world. This fact was represented by the omphalos Omphalos (or navel Omphalos), a dome-shaped stone which stood outside Apollo’s temple Ancient_Greek_temple and which also marked the spot where Apollo killed the Python Pythonidae.

The oracle The_Oracle_(The_Matrix) of Apollo at Delphi was famed throughout the Greek Ancient_Greece world and even beyond. The oracle The_Oracle_(The_Matrix) - the Pythia or priestess - would answer questions put to her by visitors wishing to be guided in their future actions. The whole process was a lengthy one, usually taking up a whole day and only carried out on specific days of the year. First the priestess would perform various actions of purification such as washing in the nearby Castalian Spring Castalian_Spring, burning laurel leaves, and drinking holy water Holy_water. Next an animal - usually a goat Goat - was sacrificed. The party seeking advice would then offer a pelanos - a sort of pie - before being allowed into the inner temple Ancient_Greek_temple where the priestess resided and gave her pronouncements, possibly in a drug or natural gas-induced state of ecstasy Religious_ecstasy.

Perhaps the most famous consultant of the Delphic oracle Delphi was Croesus, the fabulously rich King Rich_King_(sportscaster) of Lydia who, faced with a war against the Persians Achaemenid_Empire, asked the oracle Delphi’s advice. The oracle The_Oracle_(The_Matrix) stated that if Croesus went to war then a great empire would surely fall. Reassured by this, the Lydian Lydia king took on the mighty Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great. However, the Lydians Lydia were routed at Sardis and it was the Lydian empire Lydia which fell, a lesson that the oracle Delphi could easily be misinterpreted by the unwise or over-confident.

Delphi, as with the other major religious sites of Olympia Opel_Olympia, Nemea Nemean_Games, and Isthmia Isthmian_Games, held games to honour various gods of the Greek religion Ancient_Greek_religion. The Pythian Games Pythian_Games of Delphi began sometime between 591 and 585 BCE Common_Era and were initially held every eight years, with the only event being a musical competition where solo singers accompanied themselves on a kithara Cithara to sing a hymn Hymn to Apollo. Later, more musical contests and athletic events were added to the programme, and the games were held every four years with only the Olympic Games Ancient_Olympic_Games being more important. The principal prize for victors in the Games was a crown of laurel or bay leaves Laurus_nobilis.
