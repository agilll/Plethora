Ephialtes Ephialtes_of_Trachis (Greek Ancient_Greece: Ἐφιάλτης, Ephialtēs; although Herodotus spelled it as Ἐπιάλτης, Epialtes) was the son of Eurydemus (Greek Ancient_Greece: Ευρυδήμος) of Malis.[1] He betrayed his homeland, in hope of receiving some kind of reward from the Persians Achaemenid_Empire,[2] by showing the Persian Achaemenid_Empire forces a path around the allied Greek Ancient_Greece position at the pass of Thermopylae, which helped them win the Battle of Thermopylae in 480 BC.
