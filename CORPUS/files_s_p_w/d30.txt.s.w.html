The Greco-Persian Wars Greco-Persian_Wars (also often called the Persian Wars Greco-Persian_Wars) were a series of conflicts between the Achaemenid Empire of Persia Achaemenid_Empire and Greek Ancient_Greece city-states that started in 499 BC and lasted until 449 BC. The collision between the fractious political world of the Greeks Ancient_Greece and the enormous empire of the Persians Achaemenid_Empire began when Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great conquered the Greek-inhabited region of Ionia in 547 BC. In 499 BC, the tyrant Tyrant of Miletus, Aristagoras, embarked on an expedition to conquer the island of Naxos, with Persian Achaemenid_Empire support; however, the expedition was a debacle and, preempting his dismissal, Aristagoras incited all of Hellenic Ancient_Greece Asia Minor Anatolia into rebellion against the Persians Achaemenid_Empire. Aristagoras secured military support from Athens History_of_Athens and Eretria, and in 498 BC these forces helped to capture and burn the Persian Achaemenid_Empire regional capital of Sardis.
