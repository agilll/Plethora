Astyages (spelled by Herodotus as Ἀστυάγης Astyages; by Ctesias as Astyigas; by Diodorus as Aspadas; Babylonian Babylon: Ištumegu) was the last king of the Median Empire, r. 585–550 BCE Common_Era, the son of Cyaxares; he was dethroned in 550 BCE Common_Era by Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great. His name derives from the Old Iranian Iranian_languages Rishti Vaiga, which means "swinging the spear, lance-hurler."[1] In the inscriptions of Nabonidus the name is written Ishtuvegu.[2]

Astyages succeeded his father in 585 BCE Common_Era, following the Battle of Halys, which ended a five-year war between the Lydians Lydia and the Medes. He inherited a large empire, ruled in alliance with his two brothers-in-law, Croesus of Lydia and Nebuchadnezzar Nebuchadnezzar_II of Babylon, whose wife, Amytis Amytis_of_Media, Astyages' sister, was the queen for whom Nebuchadnezzar Nebuchadnezzar_II was said to have built the Hanging Gardens of Babylon.[3] However, due to recent evidence, the garden was likely built by the Assyrian Assyria King Sennacherib. Married to Aryenis, the sister of King Croesus of Lydia, to seal Cylinder_seal the treaty between the two empires, Astyages ascended to the Median Medes throne upon his father's death later that year.[4]

The ancient sources report almost nothing about Astyages’ reign, and a final judgment on his character is not possible, since Herodotus’ negative account (Astyages is represented as a cruel and despotic ruler) and Ctesias’ favorable one, are both biased.[5] According to Cyropaedia of Xenophon, after thirty-two years of relative stability, Astyages lost the support of his nobles during the war against Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great, whom Xenophon understands as being Astyages' grandson. This resulted in the founding of the Persian Achaemenid_Empire empire by Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great.[6]

The account given by the ancient Greek Ancient_Greece historian Herodotus relates that Astyages had a dream in which his daughter, Mandane, gave birth to a son who would destroy his empire. When a second dream warned Astyages of the dangers of Mandane's offspring, Astyages sent his general Harpagus to kill the child Cyrus Cyrus_the_Great.
