Pausanias Pausanias_of_Sparta (Greek Ancient_Greece: Παυσανίας; died c. 470 BC) was a Spartan regent Regent, general, and war leader for the Greeks Ancient_Greece who was suspected of conspiring with the Persian Achaemenid_Empire king, Xerxes I Xerxes_I, during the Greco-Persian Wars Greco-Persian_Wars. What is known of his life is largely according to Thucydides' History of the Peloponnesian War Peloponnesian_War, together with a handful of other classical Classical_antiquity sources.

After Leonidas Leonidas_I' death, while the king's son Pleistarchus was still in his minority, Pausanius Pausanias_(geographer) served as regent Regent of Sparta.
